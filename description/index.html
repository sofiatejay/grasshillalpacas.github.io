• Hypo-allergenic

    Unlike sheep’s wool, alpaca contains no lanolin to which some people are allergic.

  • Finer fibers

    Alpacas are selectively bred for finer fibers so garments will have fewer coarse “prickly” fibers than sheep’s wool, another source of irritation to some.

  • Fiber surface

    Sheep’s wool fibers have a scaly surface that alpaca fibers do not. This makes alpaca garments softer to the touch and more lustrous and shiny.

  • Warmth

    The combination of crimp and fineness allows the yarn to form tiny air pockets which provide insulation, making alpaca garments warmer.
